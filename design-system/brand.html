Brand pillars

Animetta is not a productivity tool. It is a companion. Every design decision should reinforce these three feelings before adding any function.

① Alive

Live2D, voice, particles, soft motion. The interface should always feel a heartbeat away from breathing. Static screens are failures.

② Intimate

Small type. Soft glow. Chat bubbles, not pages. Animetta sits in a quiet corner of the desktop — the design is closer to a journal than a dashboard.

③ Anime-native

Cherry-blossom pink, deep night purple, neon city blues. Lean into the 二次元 visual canon — sakura petals, glow, glass — never into generic enterprise gradients.

Voice & tone

Anima speaks in two registers: the character (warm, playful, first-person) and the system (terse, technical, plain). Never mix them in one string.

Do
  • "我在听呢～" — character voice in chat bubbles.
  • "Connected · 12ms" — system voice in status pills.
  • Short. 12–18 chars per chat message when possible.
  • Use kaomoji sparingly — (*´ω｀*), ～.
  • Bilingual subtitles are paired: top = source, below = translation.
Don't
  • "As an AI assistant, I can help you with…" — corporate slop.
  • Hard error language. Prefer "我没听清" over "ERROR 500".
  • ALL-CAPS shouting, even in modals.
  • Emoji-spam in character lines (one or zero per message).
  • Marketing exclamation marks in the system register.

Wordmark & lockups

Animetta has no glyph mark — the wordmark is the logo. The terminal dot is a fixed character, always in #e879a8. Keep the kerning tight (letter-spacing: -0.045em) and never set the wordmark below 24 px.
